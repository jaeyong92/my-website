THESIS

Decisive advantage is built not on a superior product, but on superior processes. The organization is the product.

I build the operational and commercial infrastructure that turns a company into an institutional, acquirable platform. My focus is on the hard problems that determine whether a company commands a premium at exit or gets discounted in due diligence.

My work is at the intersection of defense and dual-use technology.

PROFILE

Transaction Readiness: I eliminate the valuation discount levers that surface in acquisitions. Before any commitment is made, I determine whether it is commercially viable, operationally executable, and legally clean. I build the supply chain screening architecture that catches hidden adversarial ownership exposure before it becomes a due diligence problem, and the compliance infrastructure that removes the remediation costs acquirers use to compress multiples. This work applies equally to pure defense and to dual-use technology companies where the regulatory exposure is less obvious and the due diligence gap is wider.

Operations: My approach to resilience was forged in the infantry and codified through operations science and strategic finance. I build the institutional architecture that allows a technical team to scale: unit economics that survive investor scrutiny, governance frameworks that hold up in due diligence, and operations that scale without breaking. For private equity, this means building the repeatable SOPs and management infrastructure that hold value independent of any individual.

Geopolitics: My competence is built on living within societies, not just observing them. During the 2011 Arab Spring, I worked frontline security at a refugee center and assessed the human reality behind the headlines. Later, as a Diplomatic Attaché in Beijing, I bypassed the Embassy bubble to cultivate primary sources across social layers. I connect high-level strategy with ground truth.
