This experience gave me an intimate understanding of military life, including its culture, its operational realities, and the logic that shapes them. It trained me to think about building resilient systems by ensuring operations strategy - from processes and information systems to manufacturing and supply chain resilience - is inseparable from financial rigor (corporate finance, unit economics, P&L) and locked in by compliance (ITAR, export controls, government contracting). This holistic systems-thinking is how I build and sustain a decisive advantage - a perspective that applies not only to my work as an operations leader but to the urgent challenge of strengthening America's defense-industrial base.
  • Global Perspective: As a third-culture kid now based in New York City - via Seoul, Zurich, Beijing, London, Boston, and San Francisco - I navigate complex international environments and operate effectively across cultures. My past experiences in high-stakes international settings, from security work at a refugee center during the 2011 Arab Spring to serving as a diplomatic attaché in Beijing, have sharpened this perspective. My fluency in five languages (English, French, German, Korean, and Mandarin) provides a nuanced understanding of global geopolitics, while my time in Beijing offered a firsthand view of statecraft: how governments operate, how intelligence drives policy, how foreign policy is executed, and the strategic interests that define great power competition.
