Bio

My worldviews are deeply rooted in my upbringing in a family of classical musicians, further shaped by my military experience and my work in security at a refugee center during the Arab Spring of 2011. This background has cultivated capabilities in risk assessment, crisis management, and an acute understanding of human factors within high-stakes contexts. As a third-culture kid who has grown up, lived, and worked across Asia, Europe, and the US, I use my fluency in five languages to cross-reference how key players perceive events across distinct linguistic and socio-cultural frameworks.

Currently, I'm the VP Operations at Thor Dynamics, a defense technology company specializing in directed energy systems for counter-drone applications. I build and lead the company's operational engine. I specialize in the how - the essential counterpart to the what and who - which is vital for delivering advanced solutions for national security, military, and law enforcement.

Previously, I began my tenure at Rivian in Palo Alto with the strategy and corporate development team, focusing on strategic finance and contributing to the IPO. I then transitioned to the B2B team, where my focus shifted to sales & operations planning (S&OP), sales operations, order coordination with manufacturing, and RFPs for government sales within the automotive industry.

Early in my career, I served as a diplomatic attaché at the Embassy of Switzerland in Beijing, gaining direct experience in international relations and geopolitical analysis. I then transitioned into venture capital in London, focusing on early-stage investments (Seed, Series A, Series B) at the center of the global race for technological supremacy, such as robotics, sensor technologies, machine perception, SLAM, and autonomous vehicle technologies. I also co-founded and served as CFO for a privacy-focused data science startup and left after securing Series Seed funding.

Academically, I majored in business and information systems in college, though courses in political science – particularly comparative political economy – ultimately had the greatest impact on my career. At MIT, I focused on manufacturing, logistics, and supply chain management. My passion for aerospace and defense also led me to co-preside over the MIT Sloan Space Industry Club and serve as a fundraiser for the MIT Rocket Team.
